ARIA v5.0’s satellite audit confirms a catastrophic sequence: 
*   **Act 1 (June 15):** Baseline imagery shows a stable, forested Matai'an valley. 
*   **Act 2 (Sept 11):** Detection of a **1.022 km² barrier lake**, confirming the hazard was fully formed weeks before the breach.

**2. Pre-Breach Warning Window**
A **64-day window** (July 21 – Sept 23) existed for intervention. Had ARIA v5.0 been operational, the Sept 11 detection would have provided a 12-day high-alert lead time prior to the breach, likely saving critical infrastructure.

Our W3/W7 modeling over-indexed on Hualien City’s network centrality, resulting in **zero** alerts for Guangfu nodes. While Hualien City remained safe, Guangfu suffered **4 out of 5 critical hits**, including the Station and Township Office. Furthermore, **28 W3 shelters** were found to be within 200m of the landslide source zone, highlighting a dangerous mapping blind spot.
